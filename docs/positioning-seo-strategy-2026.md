# Emerito: Positioning & SEO Content Strategy

**Date:** July 2026 · **Owner:** Miguel Zuluaga
**Decision context:** Reposition from "behavior-change studio" to **creative content studio**. Behavioral science becomes a background method (methodology page only). Primary acquisition market: **international / English**.

---

## 1. Positioning

### The market gap
The English-market training-video space has split into two camps:

- **AI-native / volume shops** (Colossyan, Synthesia, Knowlify): $3K videos, 72-hour turnaround, template aesthetics. They win on price and speed.
- **Premium craft studios** (Yum Yum Videos, Giant Ant, Broadcast2World): fewer, higher-investment projects, narrative-first.

Nobody in either camp owns this claim: **training and brand content built around original characters that people actually remember and act on.** Generic training video is the thing employees click through. Character-driven story is the thing they quote at lunch. That's Emerito's lane, premium craft, with a proof point competitors can't copy (real results like Trabajo Seguro en Alturas).

### Positioning statement
> **Emerito is a creative content studio that makes training, safety, and brand content people actually watch, remember, and act on, through original characters, story, and craft.**

- **Category:** creative content studio (not "behavior-change studio", not "video production company")
- **Lead offer:** content for training / safety / culture + branded content & campaigns
- **Differentiator:** original character IP + narrative craft + measurable behavior outcomes
- **Behavioral science:** the *why it works*, not the headline. Lives on the methodology page and inside case-study results. Never in the hero, nav labels, or meta titles.
- **Commercial edge (use in sales, not in hero):** Medellín = US time zones, bilingual team, premium output at nearshore economics. Never say "cheap"; say "senior craft without US-studio overhead."

### One-line messaging by page (EN site)
| Page | Message |
|---|---|
| Home | Training and brand content people actually remember. Original characters, real stories, measurable results. |
| Work | Content that changed what people do, not just what they watched. |
| Services | From safety training series to brand characters and campaigns. |
| Methodology | Why our content works: story + craft, informed by behavioral science. *(only place science leads)* |
| About/Team | A character-driven studio in Medellín working with teams across the Americas. |

### What changes on the current site
1. Hero: replace "Cambiamos cómo actúa la gente" framing with content/creativity-first headline; keep the outcome ("people act differently") as the *result* line, not the identity.
2. Pillars: "Contenido Creativo" moves to 01 and becomes the umbrella; "Ciencia del Comportamiento" folds into methodology; "Pensamiento de Futuro" becomes a service ingredient, not a pillar.
3. `/en/` becomes the primary acquisition site: full service pages, all case studies translated, blog in English first (translate to ES after).

## 2. Target client & search intent

**Primary buyer:** L&D / HSE / Internal Comms managers at US & international mid-market and enterprise companies (manufacturing, construction, energy, logistics) who need training content that isn't ignored.
**Secondary buyer:** Marketing / brand managers wanting character-driven branded content and campaigns.

What 2026 buyers actually search and evaluate on (from market research):

- Bottom-funnel service queries: "safety training video production company", "animated training video company", "[format] for [industry]"
- Comparison/cost queries: "training video production cost", "best training video companies", "live-action vs animated training"
- Enterprise qualifiers they scan pages for: sector experience, LMS-ready delivery (SCORM/xAPI), consistent quality across a portfolio, end-to-end service

Commercial-intent keywords convert ~10x informational ones. **Strategy: bottom-funnel first, then build the informational cluster around it.**

Pillar B is smaller in search volume but is what makes Pillar A pages *convert*, characters are the visual proof of difference.

## 4. Prioritized content plan (first 90 days)

Order = build order. Money pages before articles.

| # | Piece | Target query | Funnel | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Service page: Training video production | training video production company | BOFU | Core money page |
| 2 | Service page: Safety training videos | safety training video production company | BOFU | Strongest case study fit |
| 3 | Case study (EN, results-led rewrite): Working Safely at Heights | safety training video case study | BOFU | Proof engine, lead with the behavior metric |
| 4 | Article: How much does a training video cost in 2026? | training video production cost | MOFU | Highest-traffic commercial query in the niche; anchor your pricing honestly |
| 5 | Article: Animated vs live-action training videos | animated vs live action training video | MOFU | Comparison intent, feeds both service pages |
| 6 | Service page: Animated training videos | animated training video company | BOFU | Second money page |
| 7 | Article: Why employees ignore safety training (and what fixes it) | why safety training doesn't work | MOFU | The behavioral-science content, repackaged as a buyer pain, science as substance, not identity |
| 8 | Industry page: Construction & work at heights | construction safety training videos | BOFU | Direct case-study match |
| 9 | Article: 12 training video examples that actually changed behavior | training video examples | TOFU | Link magnet; include own work + others |
| 10 | Service page: Brand characters | brand character animation studio | BOFU | Pillar B anchor |
| 11 | Article: What a brand character does that a logo can't | brand mascot ROI / brand character marketing | MOFU | Pillar B feeder |
| 12 | Article: SCORM & LMS-ready video content: buyer's checklist | scorm compliant training videos | MOFU | Enterprise qualifier, signals you speak L&D |

Cadence after day 90: 2 articles/month, alternating pillars; every article internally links to its money page; every case study rewritten results-first.

### Content rules
- Every piece answers a query a *buyer* types, not a peer. No "behavioral science explains X" thought-leadership until pillars rank.
- Case studies lead with the number (e.g. incident/compliance metric), then the creative, then, briefly, the method.
- English first; translate winners to Spanish. `hreflang` pairs on everything.
- Publish honest pricing ranges (market anchors: $2,000–$8,000/finished minute for 2D animation; $25K+ for multi-module programs). Pricing transparency is a top BOFU converter and most craft studios hide it.

## 5. Technical SEO checklist (ties to redesign)
- [ ] Finish Step 5 of the build plan: sitemap, meta titles/descriptions per page, OG images
- [ ] New meta pattern: `[Service] | Emerito, Creative Content Studio` (drop behavior-change wording from titles/descriptions)
- [ ] `hreflang` es/en pairs; `/en/` gets equal crawl priority
- [ ] Schema: `Organization`, `Service`, `VideoObject` on case studies, `FAQPage` on cost article
- [ ] Case-study video pages: transcripts + captions (indexable text for video-heavy pages)
- [ ] Redirects if any URL slugs change in redesign

## 6. Measurement
- North star: qualified inquiries from organic (EN form fills mentioning training/brand content)
- Leading: rankings for the 5 BOFU money-page keywords; impressions on cost/comparison articles
- Review at 90 days: double down on whichever pillar generates inquiries
